## Topic Pitch

For our Unit 3 project, we will use the **CDC PLACES: Local Data for Better Health** dataset
(2024 county-level release). PLACES is a collaboration between the CDC, the Robert Wood Johnson
Foundation, and the CDC Foundation that provides modeled estimates of 40 public health measures
—including chronic disease rates, preventive care use, health risk behaviors, disability
prevalence, and health-related social needs—for every county in the United States. The dataset
contains over 3,000 county observations and more than 40 variables, well above the project
minimums. It is publicly available and freely downloadable from the CDC's open data portal at:

> https://data.cdc.gov/500-Cities-Places/PLACES-Local-Data-for-Better-Health-County-Data-20/swc5-untb

We plan to explore three core questions: (1) Which US counties have the highest rates of
preventable chronic conditions such as diabetes, obesity, and high blood pressure, and where are
they concentrated geographically? (2) Is there a measurable relationship between preventive care
usage (e.g., annual checkups, cholesterol screening) and chronic disease outcomes at the county
level? (3) How do health risk behaviors like smoking and physical inactivity correlate with
poor health outcomes across regions? Our interactive Shiny dashboard will let users filter by
state and health measure, compare counties side-by-side, and explore a choropleth map of the US.
This dataset is entirely aggregate and contains no individually identifiable information, so
there are no ethical concerns about individual privacy.

## Data Source
- **URL:** https://data.cdc.gov/500-Cities-Places/PLACES-Local-Data-for-Better-Health-County-Data-20/swc5-untb/about_data
- **Format:** CSV (downloadable directly from the portal)
- **Size:** ~3,143 rows (one per US county) × 40+ variables
- **License:** Public domain / US Government open data
